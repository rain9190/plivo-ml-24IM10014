The thesis

Two questions decided everything. Where is the corpus wasting compute? and What is the binding constraint — parameters or steps? Measurement answered both. A third of the byte budget is Devanagari, which a byte-level tokenizer spends three tokens per character on, so the tokenizer was the first and largest lever. And a deliberate capacity experiment (Run 3) made the score worse, proving the run is optimization-limited, not parameter-limited — which redirected all further effort toward faster convergence inside the step budget rather than a bigger model.

The differentiator — script-aware tokenization

Plain BPE is greedy on frequency. Because English is 67% of the corpus, English byte-pairs win almost every merge and Devanagari is starved — measured on dev, English compressed to ~3.16 characters per token but Hindi to only ~1.58, despite Hindi being where bytes are most expensive. The naive fix (reallocate merges toward Hindi at fixed vocab) was zero-sum: Hindi improved but English degraded, total tokens flat. The real fix was to expand the budget under the cap rather than steal from English — vocab 2304 with a protected Devanagari pass — cutting total dev tokens 2.8% while holding English and improving Hindi to ~1.74 characters per token. Small bpb gain, but a measured, defensible mechanism that byte-BPE structurally cannot reach.

Human vs. machine

The code itself — the BPE trainer, the script-aware allocation, the training-loop edits, the HTML — was written with an AI coding assistant. What is human is everything that decided what to try and why: reading the corpus to find the Devanagari cost, forming each hypothesis, choosing to test capacity and reading its failure as proof of a step-limit, diagnosing the naive script-aware attempt as zero-sum and engineering the budget-expanding fix, and stopping each lever at its knee rather than chasing a number. The assistant produced a strong baseline of code; the reasoning, the experimental discipline, and the interpretation of every result are mine.

Human — direction, hypotheses, diagnosis, decisions
